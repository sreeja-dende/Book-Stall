Chemical engineering is a branch of engineering that combines principles of chemistry, physics, mathematics, biology, and economics to efficiently use, produce, design, transport, and transform energy and materials. Chemical engineers are primarily involved in processes where chemicals are transformed or produced, whether in manufacturing, research, or development of new technologies.

### Core Subjects in Chemical Engineering:

1. **Fluid Mechanics**:

- Focuses on the behavior of fluids (liquids and gases) in motion or at rest. It covers the principles of fluid flow, pressure, viscosity, and the design of equipment like pumps, valves, and reactors that handle fluids.

2. **Thermodynamics**:

- Studies the relationships between heat, work, energy, and the properties of matter. It plays a critical role in understanding and optimizing chemical processes, particularly in energy transfer and conversion.

3. **Heat and Mass Transfer**:

- Deals with the movement of heat and mass in different phases (solid, liquid, and gas). This subject is essential for the design of equipment like heat exchangers, distillation columns, and reactors.

4. **Chemical Reaction Engineering**:

- Focuses on understanding the rates and mechanisms of chemical reactions, the design of chemical reactors, and the optimization of reactions for large-scale production.

5. **Process Control**:

- Involves the study of systems used to control industrial processes. Chemical engineers use control theory to design systems that regulate temperature, pressure, flow, and composition in chemical plants.

6. **Materials Science**:

- Examines the properties and behavior of materials, particularly those used in chemical processing, like metals, polymers, and ceramics. Understanding these materials is essential for equipment design and selection.

7. **Separation Processes**:

- Covers techniques used to separate components of mixtures, such as filtration, distillation, membrane processes, adsorption, and centrifugation. These methods are essential in producing purified products from raw materials.

8. **Process Design and Simulation**:

- Focuses on designing chemical processes and systems, from conceptualization to optimization. Engineers use simulation software to model and predict the behavior of processes.

9. **Environmental Engineering and Sustainability**:

- Focuses on reducing environmental impact by designing processes that minimize waste, energy consumption, and pollution. This subject also involves recycling, waste treatment, and sustainable practices in chemical industries.

10. **Biochemical Engineering** (in some programs):

- Combines chemical engineering principles with biological sciences. This field is important for bioprocessing, such as the production of pharmaceuticals, biofuels, and food.

11. **Mathematics and Computational Methods**:

- Chemical engineers use advanced mathematics (calculus, differential equations, linear algebra, etc.) to model and analyze complex systems, and computational methods to simulate and optimize processes.

### Advanced Topics:

1. **Catalysis**: The study of catalysts and catalytic processes used to accelerate chemical reactions.

2. **Nanotechnology**: Focuses on the manipulation of matter on a molecular or atomic scale, often applied in material design and pharmaceuticals.

3. **Safety Engineering**: Deals with understanding and preventing accidents, designing safe chemical plants, and ensuring process safety.

4. **Energy Systems**: Focuses on the design and optimization of systems for energy production, storage, and conversion.

Chemical engineering is a multidisciplinary field, and its core subjects form the foundation for solving real-world problems in manufacturing, pharmaceuticals, energy production, and more.
