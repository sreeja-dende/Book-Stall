⚡Electrical and Electronics Engineering⚡

**Electrical and Electronics Engineering (EEE)** focuses on the study of electrical systems, electronic devices, and electromagnetism. It combines core electrical concepts with modern electronics, making it a versatile branch of engineering.

### ⚡ **Core Subjects in EEE:**

1. **Electrical Circuits and Networks:**

- Kirchhoff's laws, network theorems, and circuit analysis.

2. **Electromagnetic Field Theory:**

- Electric and magnetic fields, Maxwell's equations, and wave propagation.

3. **Electrical Machines:**

- DC machines, transformers, synchronous and induction machines — essential for power generation and motors.

4. **Power Systems:**

- Generation, transmission, and distribution of electric power, including grid systems.

5. **Control Systems:**

- Feedback systems, PID controllers, and stability analysis.

6. **Power Electronics:**

- Semiconductor devices like SCR, MOSFETs, and IGBTs used for power conversion.

7. **Analog and Digital Electronics:**

- Op-amps, logic gates, flip-flops, and microprocessors — similar to ECE but with a focus on power applications.

8. **Signals and Systems:**

- Time and frequency domain analysis, Fourier transforms, and system responses.

9. **Microprocessors and Microcontrollers:**

- 8085, 8051, and their applications in embedded systems.

10. **Renewable Energy Systems:**

- Solar, wind, and other sustainable energy sources and their integration into the grid.
